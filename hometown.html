Pahang

Pahang is a big state in Peninsular Malaysia with 1,642,700 population
and Kuantan is the capital of the state of Pahang.
Pahang stretches from a region of hills in the west to one
of Peninsula Malaysia's most stunning and longest coastlines in the east.
Pahang has cool green highlands, rain forests, hill resorts,
peaceful fishing villages, large lengths of sandy beaches, intriguing caverns, and beautiful lakes.
Moreover, has a great deal to offer visitors, both domestic and international.
Specialising in songket, a unique hand-woven brocade of gold and silver, Pahang has a rich cultural legacy.
Visitors can visit the Teluk Chempedak, Sungai Karang, and Cherating handicraft centres.
These centres sell woodcarvings, batik, shell crafts, and household goods fashioned from pandanus leaves.
